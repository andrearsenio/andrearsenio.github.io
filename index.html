About me

Welcome! I am a researcher and consultant based in Maputo, Mozambique. In my various projects I advocate and support integrated approaches for the Urban Water Cycle - bridging sanitation services, water supply and water resources management - as a sustainable way to address water security and environmental pollution which are serious challenges for populations and decision makers across the Global South.

From 2009 until 2013 I worked at KWR - Watercycle Research Institute while doing my PhD within the Department of Water Management at the Delft University of Technology; since 2014 I have been involved several projects throughout Mozambique, to where I moved in early 2017.

On the right you can find a sample of the projects where I am currently involved in, and if you want to get in touch please check the contact info at the bottom of this page.

My projects

Sustainable water for urbanizing Maputo, Mozambique

Period: 2014-2019
Partners: TU Delft, IHE Delft, UEM, VEI, FIPAG
Description: NWO-funded research project, through their Urbanizing Deltas of the World program, whereby with the support of four Mozambican PhDs, we have been developing tools, models and approaches that will enable the Mozambican WaSH sector to include water reuse in their water resources plans.
Peer-reviewed publications: Improving health in cities through systems approaches for urban water management and Mitigation Potential of Sanitation Infrastructure on Groundwater Contamination by Nitrate in Maputo
