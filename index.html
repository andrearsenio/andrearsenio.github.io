About me

Welcome! I am a researcher and consultant based in Maputo, Mozambique and I like to use my knowledge in water and wastewater, my expertise in education and keen interest in mathematical modelling and systems thinking to support the local WaSH sector.

I am a strong advocate of integrated approaches for the Urban Water Cycle, bridging sanitation services, water supply and water resources management which, I believe, are the most sustainable way forward. I also believe that capacity building and innovation can greatly support the Global South in leap-frogging development.

From 2009 until 2013 I worked at KWR while doing my PhD at Delft University of Technology and since 2014 I have been involved several research, capacity building and innovation projects in Mozambique, to where I moved in early 2017. To get in touch please check my contact info at the bottom of this page.

My projects

Sustainable water for urbanizing Maputo, Mozambique

Period: 2014-2019
Partners: TU Delft, IHE Delft, UEM, VEI, FIPAG
Description: NWO-funded research project, through their Urbanizing Deltas of the World program, whereby with the support of four Mozambican PhDs, we have been developing tools, models and approaches that will enable the Mozambican WaSH sector to include water reuse in their water resources plans.
Peer-reviewed publications: Improving health in cities through systems approaches for urban water management and Mitigation Potential of Sanitation Infrastructure on Groundwater Contamination by Nitrate in Maputo
